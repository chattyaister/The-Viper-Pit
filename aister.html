Psychological Assessment

The bird, at a glance, is just a pleasant, friendly face, quite charismatic, adequately social, relatively chipper, she fits into most social settings like a charm. She appears happy and easygoing, and many find that the bird is easy to open up to due to her seemingly lax, thoughtful, and charming nature. Many find themselves comfortable around her, chattering away about most mundane to the most personal things with her without much thought to it.

Sometimes, however, the bird appears a hint absent, like her mind wanders elsewhere to places unknown. She tunes out entirely, her attention fully elsewhere during these times as she’s caught in the messy tangles of her own mind. Pulling her back from these moments isn’t difficult, any external stimuli seems to draw her attention back with just a moment of missed time and a hint of confusion- only to return right to her usual pleasant self.

Despite her friendly and pleasant facade, the bird in reality, harbors a generally more detached personality. She does not care for others in the way a friend, or a lover, or a family, might care for each other. She does not connect with others, never seeing a person as more than just an acquaintance, just another person, just another piece in the chessboard she has no hand in playing. Though she easily calls people friends, she does not feel the connection, or the pull, associated with friendships, just as she calls the Viper Pit family without a shred of attachment to it. People to her were like water, rushing by, flowing without pause. And she simply hadn’t the thought to cup her hands to catch them.

The bird is indifferent to people, to places, to things, to circumstances, through and through, as she has never learned to be otherwise.

She remembers small details about people, like a person's preferences in food, or the drinks and snacks they like, or little comments made long time ago. It makes people around her feel seen, contributing to her friendliness.

She often makes herself useful, helping out wherever she can, whether it be professors or other students.

Bad Habits

Despite her easygoing and friendly front, she is surprisngly withdrawn- She learns of other people but never lets herself be known. She has a habit of deflecting personal questions, redirecting them to talk about other things instead. She seems to dislike making herself a topic.

She is independednt to a fault, refusing to rely on other people and often attempt to resolve all problems that come her way without a helping hand. This leaves her often exhausted when problems arise.

She blends with any crowd well, altering herself depending on which group of people she is with. This makes it difficult to know which is her 'true' self, which of her thoughts are from herself, and which belong to the faces she puts on.

Secrets

She's a spy. Against Beacon. From the Viper Pit. She prays Headmaster Ozpin will never find out, if he hasn't already.

Her identity exists solely as the "Viper Pit Scale". She does not know what she is without that role, other than lost.

Becoming trapped/losing her freedom. She is a bird, and birds are meant to fly. She is afraid of the day she can no longer fly of her own accord.

Combat Data

Range: Close-Ranged Combat

Notable Traits: The Reaper specializes in combat with her sycthe specifically, limiting her to close-ranged combat. The glock only comes out if absolutely necessary or if the situation prefers it. She is exceptionally skilled in combat with her scythe, using the gravity dust infused within her weapon to pivot the trajectory of her swings with ease and increase and decrease the weight of her attacks as needed.

Threat Level: Unclear

Asphodel

An extension of her self, she wields the scythe with comfort and ease.

Asphodel is an ornately designed scythe, a little taller than Ai herself, adorned in crystals and feathers. The weapon does not change forms at all, nor minimize in size aside from the blade folding in towards the handle- the Reaper carries the weapon in it's full size everywhere she goes, nestled carefully between her wings.

Despite it's large size, the scythe is fairly lightweight due to the blade being infused with gravity dust, and the gravity dust crystals embedded into the weapon at various points throughout. Using the gravity dust, the Reaper swings the weapon without much effort, activating the dust at various points to add weight to her attacks or change the trajectory of her weapon. She has been known to throw the entire freaking weapon at her enemy, miss, only to recall the weapon with the dust back to her into the back of her cackling enemy's head.

She wears a pair of sleeves, which the inner lining is woven with gravity dust, that allows her to recall Asphodel to her.

She has a glock. A plain, non-transformative, no dust infused, glock. holds 17+1 rounds.

Dust Usage

Gravity Dust

Adorns a significant portion of Asphodel and the inner linings of her detachable sleeves. The Gravity dust is used for a variety of purposes, from making her blows heavier to making the weapon itself lighter to carry and swing, to pull the weapon towards her in the event the weapon is thrown for some explicable reason.

Strenghts

Highly Adaptable. She seems to adapt well to any and all unexpected situations thrown her way calmly, always appearing to know what to do and know what she's doing, making her a quite reliable ally.

Keen Sight. She has an exceptionally keen sense of sight, especially great at tracking movement, making it difficult to lose her even from a distance for as long as her target continues moving.

Weathered. During her time in the Vermillion Pact and the Viper Pit, she has had the opportunity to experience all sorts of live combat, mostly against other humans and fauni more so than Grimm. Having trained from an age where she could barely hold up a weapon, she has learned to treat the weapon as an extension of herself.

Weaknesses

Ranged Combat. She's only got a glock on her for the occasion where she needs to engage in long-ranged combat. But she'd really rather not, if she can help it. The glock is a last resort if she finds she simply cannot close distance with her enemies.

Team Play. Given that she's always played solo during her time at the two Organizations she had been a part of, she is not apt at working with a team. She is just not used to having people at her back, it just wasn't how life was for her.

Protection. When others need her protection, such as a downed ally, or a civillian, she seems to lose her nerves, her movements becoming more and more predictable and takes risks the enemeies are bound to exploit.

Soul-Blossom

A single person at a time can be designated her ‘soul-blossom’ be it an enemy or an ally. When a target is selected, a gaze their way starts a sprout that blooms against the center of their chest. This blossom does not cause any harm to the target, and cannot be interacted with by a person other than Ai herself.

The Soul-Blossom grows as the target takes damage, and depending on the whom the host of the blossom is, as well as whom the attacks are coming from, stacks buffs within it that can be activated via harvesting.

Stages of Soul-Blossom

[ Bud - Minor Damages, ~20% of aura broken ]

  • Minor Buffs, 1 type

[ Sprout - Moderate Damages ~40% of aura broken ]

  • Minor Buffs, 2 types or;
  • Moderate Buffs, 1 type

[ Bloom - Major Damages ~60% of aura broken ]

  • Moderate Buffs, 2 types or;
  • Major Buffs, 1 types

[ Blossom - Significant Damages 60%+ of aura broken ]

  • Major Buffs, 2 types

Types of Buffs

Enemy-Host vs. Reaper-Damage

  • Examples of Offensive Buffs
    • Increased Strength
    • Heightened Senses
    • Improved Weapon Handling
    • Improved Reaction Time

Enemy-Host vs. Ally-Damage

  • Examples of Supportive Buffs
    • Increased Speed
    • Increased Threat Sense
    • Improved Agility
    • Improved Acrobatics

Ally-Host vs. Enemy-Damage

  • Examples of Defensive Buffs
    • Increased Aura Durability
    • Increased Pain Tolerance
    • Reduced Stagger/Improved Balance
    • Enhanced Endurance

The Soul-Blossom may be harvested by the Reaper and the Reaper only. She either grabs it and pulls it free, or slices it free with her weapon, requiring close-range contact to harvest the flower.

While she can look away from the target of her Soul-Blossom, she must maintain her attention on the target for the flower to bloom from damage the target takes.

The flower being harvested does not cause the target any harm.

She can target only one person at a time as the bearer of the Soul-Blossom, and moving targets will cause the soul blossom to ‘restart’ from its bud form.

The Soul-Blossom, once free of its host, lasts for 3 minutes, before it withers and disperses. It must be used within that timeframe otherwise the flower and the buff disappears.

The buffs last for 10 minutes after the ‘harvest’ of the soul blossom.

The flower wilts and disperses when the targets aura shield is broken, making it unviable for harvest.

The Flower, upon harvest, maintains it’s spectral flower-like form, and once it is crushed in the hands of it’s “user” usually the Reaper herself, the buff is applied. While the Soul-Blossom can only be harvested by the Reaper, it can be given once harvested to another person to apply its effects onto someone else.
