Psychological Assessment

Aluran is a calm, quiet man of few words- and when he does speak he is gentle and soft spoken. His interactions with others are generally polite and well mannered, and he seems genuine in these aspects of his personality.

However beneath this pleasant facade, he is fundamentally broken- Screams and cries in his presence are met with his usual pleasantries and gentle words and reassurance, as if it is ordinary. He seems to lack a moral compass, and if he has one it appears askew, as he does not seem affected in a natural way by the woes and plights of others.

He appears unbothered by chaos, and when his allies engage in combat within his presence he simply stands back and observes, without engaging himself. He prefers not to engage in fights as long as he does not feel the need to engage.

Likes

New ideas and revelations. He enjoys coming across anything new and interesting, giving himself something to occupy his time with.

New subjects. the more the merrier. He’s always short on subjects to use.m, and will secure new ones happily when given the chance.?

Tea. A cup of tea to calm the nerves. Bitter or sweet, he enjoys them either way.

Organization clean, clutter free desks, labeled vials, things laid out in the exact order he wants them. He is tidy and organized, keeping his surroundings immaculate.

Dislikes

Being Disturbed. he hates being forcefully pulled away from what he is currently doing, whether it be from experimenting or tidying his lab or sowing his glove.

Being seen. he hates the feeling of someone “seeing” him in the sense that they understand him.

Silence. the suffocating, utter absence of noise. In the silence is when his thoughts are most rampant.

Good Habits

Observant, although the man does not engage in combat he will watch, taking notice of flaws in the enemy’s fighting style.

Bad Habits

He is often medicated with his own experimental concoctions, to quiet the noise in his mind.

Secrets

Despite… everything, he enjoys the company of people, be it Wisteria, Kiel, strangers or his subjects.

He has a rough time every time someone dies from his experiments. He continues nonetheless.

He has an intense fear of abandonment, making him rather blindly loyal to those that stay despite it all.

At the same time, he has a fear of attachment, and while he often makes acquaintances, he rarely opens his doors beyond that.

Recovered Origin Records

Dad

His father was an abuser. His mother ran from home without him when he was just 6. The young boy understood, but that didn’t make the ache any less intense. When his mother ran from home, his own became the basement, limited to as far as the chains that wound his neck would extend. His father reasoned that he wouldn’t lose his son too, not his own flesh and blood. At first, the boy pleaded with his father, cried and begged to be let out. He pulled at the chains, scratched at his skin, tore at his flesh, leaving jagged wounds and scars that circled around his neck. His nails scraped at the door that always remained firmly shut to his cries, his endlessly unheard.

No one looked for the boy. Everyone knew the mother had ran, and it was always assumed the boy had gone with her. But he hadn’t, and so the boy fell through the cracks, invisible in the eyes of the world.

When his father was angry, he brought the boy pain and lashings, enough to break his aura and more. He’d been punched, been kicked, been beaten and thrown, skin torn, bones broken organs ruptured. But the tenacious boy lived. His father would beat him, and then come to apologize to the boy and tend to his wounds.

When his father wasn’t angry, he was gentle. He came down, talked to the boy with reassuring words and apologies, spoke of how things would be alright, how his son was his world and everything that mattered to him. He mended his wounds and spoke of how he couldn’t bear losing him too, how this was for his own good. He brought him food, brought him drinks. Brought him toys and gentle company, tell him stories about his plights as a scientist.

The boy treasured these moments with all his heart.

Here, in the quiet of the basement, hours turned to days, days turned to weeks, weeks turned to months and months to years. It was year two the boy stopped crying, as he learned his pleas and cries reached no ears that mattered. He grew quieter with passing days, as he learned that his voice did not matter. He’d simply sit in the basement, pulling absentmindedly at his chains, awaiting his fathers good mood to bring him more stories and comfort.

Ten years.

Alone

It was ten years that he spent alone in the confines his father had put him in. Ten years, the small basement his only world. Ten years, it took his father to slip up in the most minuscule way he could. He had left the boy in a fit of rage, and had stormed from the room, the door slightly ajar behind him. And the boy watched with interest as lights and shadows moved beyond the door ajar, seeing a world beyond the world he called his own.

A longing grew. A longing to see the light and shadows move up close to be where his father was. But his chains held tight. He pulled at the chains, reaching his hand to the open doors. Beyond that door, was his father. He wanted to see him, go to him, to listen to the stories his father told. He longed to leave the confines of his basement.

His father hadn’t visited him in three days. And in those three days of his fathers absence his longing grew- enough, to for the first time in his life, activate his semblance. On the third day, he reached once again to the door, longing to leave, to step into the light, to find his absent father. Shadows rose around him, wrapping him in the gentle embrace like that of his father when he spoke quietly to the boy. And when the shadows folded, he had vanished, manifesting again just a few feet away in a swarm of black wisp and tendrils, standing, at the foot of the stairs, no longer bound by the chains that had kept him for years. The sensation was odd, and the boy would rub his scarred neck, looking back to the chains that had settled on the ground with a clatter behind him. He’d glance down at the chains, up at the stairs. And then back down to the chains, before turning around and heading cautiously up the stairs.

The door creaked open further, mismatched eyes peeking through the crack.

“… dad?”

His voice trembled lightly, afraid he wouldn’t hear a reply. The call went unanswered. He crept up into the room- a kitchen- and he’d begin turning the room upside down, in search of food, water, his father- anything. He’d go through the cabinets, and fridge and drawers, eat and drink what he found. He’d make his way from the kitchen to the dining room to the living room to the bedrooms, searching desperately. Where, where was he? Where was his father?

Hours of searching every room, every closet, every crevasse the boy had exhausted himself. His father was not there. The boy absentmindedly would head back to the kitchen, down the stairs, into his basement, where he would lull himself to sleep as he had always done.

Four more days passed. The boy spent his time growing more and more anxious, waiting for a father who did not come homes

Stay

*click*

The sound of the door opening. The boy perked up from behind the emptied fridge to the unfamiliar sound, and he stood, picking up a knife on the way to where the noise had come from. And as he turned the corner into the foyer, his father entered the home, and the boy brightened

“Dad-"

The tired man looked up in surprise, his lab gown spotted in blood and his aura already mangled. The boy ran towards his father, beaming brightly and into his arms he went, the father catching him and lifting him into a hug.

“How did you-“

the knife entered his back. He sputtered as he dropped the boy back down to the floor, who held the bloodied knife in his hand, he’d glance down at the knife, and then to his father. Stab. The knife entered once more into his chest, precisely where the heart beat. The boy blinked watching his father collapse to the ground, blood pouring from the gaping wound he had created.

“… stay with me.”

The boy whispered under his breath. He’d lift the man’s arm, wrapping it around his shoulder as he pulled the man, with all his might, into the kitchen, down the stairs, into the basement, leaving behind a stream of red and gore along the ground. He’d place his father upright against the wall, huddling himself close to the man barely breathing, he’d tell the man stories of what he had done in his fathers absence, the foods he ate, and the things he’s seen. He told of the dreams he dreamt, and the sounds he’s heard, and the things he smelled. All the unfamiliar things he’d experienced while the man was gone. And there the man drew his final breaths, his arms wrapped haphazardly around his killer, the boy chattering happily away to his father.

Runaway

It’d be a week before anyone came looking for the man. The neighbors had taken notice of the man’s absences, and finding it strange, they had wandered into the home to find the place lined with dried blood and upturned furniture. When huntsmen and huntresses were called, they expected it to be a robbery gone wrong- but they had found the boy still in the basement, curled up besides his fathers decaying body asleep, pieces rapidly fit into shape. It took ten long years to learn that the invisible boy had never left. The huntress and huntsmen took the young boy in, but it did not take long for the boy to disappear once again, this time in search of his mother.

When he found his mother, he’d learn that she’d turned her life around since running from home, with a new husband and a new child- his half brother. He watched from a distance as they lived their lives happily, quietly, perfectly. When the boy showed up at their doorstep, the mother recognized him immediately. She burst into tears, hands caressing his face, thumbs running over the boys mismatched eyes- she knew those eyes. Her child’s her son, her past she had left behind. She hugged him, welcomed him inside, and the boy smiled.

Within a week, the family would be found deceased, their bodies neatly sat upright on their sofa, uneaten food laid neatly out in the table before them. The boy sat among the bodies, staring absentmindedly ahead- his family was here, physically besides him- yet, they were gone. He felt empty

This time, the boy was taken into custody. The first time he had been found, it had been exorbitantly clear what had happened. A child, abused, had killed their abuser as a means to escape the abuse. It was easy to explain. Label. But this time, his thought process, his motive, it was all unclear. And the boy did not speak. Not a day into custody, the boy would disappear yet again from the grasp of the huntsmen. And this time’s the boy took to the streets.

Escape

He never stopped feeling empty. The experiments began with stray animals that he found. At first he sought to quell the pains he felt, the anguish over those that wouldn’t stay. He sought ways to numb his emotions, numb his feelings, calm his thoughts. When that failed he began to seek ways to strengthen people, believing that broken people, in broken situations, make broken choices. He’d find a way to make people… better. Stronger. Unbroken. It was during this time, Wisteria found him. She found his obsession… Amusing. Entertaining. Useful.

“You’re beautifully broken.”

She’d take him under her wings, providing him with the company he so desired for as long as he would assist her, assist her in doing the things she enjoyed- starting with dismantling an organization that she found meddlesome. And so he would find his way into the organization, helping her take it apart bit by bit from the inside, while she took it apart from the outside.

It was under her, he’d develop his signature drugs, “Hush” and “Venom”, prototypes to his ideals, that the Viper Pit would make good use of.

To this day, he continues his experiments.

Combat Data

Range: Non-Combat

The man is not a fighter by any means. But when he does engage in battle his attacks are precise and calculated, aiming for weak spots in the enemy’s defense, scalpel at the ready..

Notable Traits: Stealth, assassinations, single lethal blows

Threat Level: Severe

Vials of Acid and Scalpels

The doctor has vials containing a potent acid of some kind, and when thrown, the vials shatter and spread it’s content on the target. The acid burns at aura shields, and is potent enough to eat through clothing and skin if unprotected.

He also carries ordinary sharp medical instruments, for precise cuts.

Dust Usage

Steam Dust

He has some steam dust on hand always, that he uses to create a smokescreen effect to prevent enemies from attacking accurately and hide himself away.

Fire Dust

pure, fine powdered fire dust in a vial. Perfect for causing minor explosions.

Strenghts

Thinking outside the box. He’s known to think up unique and surprisingly effective solutions to problems he comes across, which can sometimes come in quite handy in dire situations.

Silence. he is naturally quiet, his voice, his footsteps, and overall ambient noise he creates. This makes him apt at stealthing. He is seen standing around quietly undetected, quietly listening in on information that would otherwise go unshared.

Weaknesses

Strength. He is quite weak strength-wise, and could not best the average huntsman or huntress when it comes to raw power.

I’d rather not… He is not fond of engaging in combat, never initiates combat himself- only defends himself if a fight unfolds. Even so, he is more likely to escape combat rather than actively engage.

Semblance: Shadow’s Embrace

Aluran’s semblance allows him to, in a wisp of shadows and tendrils, transport themselves and an ally he is in contact with, if needed to a new location that is within his line of sight. When teleporting, black-violet ink-like wisps wrap around him, before dropping down and fading to nothing.

Each teleportation costs approximately 5% of his aura per body teleported- in essence, he can teleport by himself 20 consecutive times before running out of aura, or if he has one other person, he will only be able to teleport 10 times consecutively before running out of aura.

They cannot teleport where they cannot see- blocking his line of sight is an effective way to prevent his teleportation semblance. A blindfold is enough to inhibit the use of his semblance.

The maximum range of a single teleportation is approximately 100 ft from his current location.
