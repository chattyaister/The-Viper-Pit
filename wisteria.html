Subject File 001-B — Origin Record

"Silence is a little bland, don't you think?"

Subject Photograph

Distinguishing Features

The Viper has a long, white tail, covered in hard, almost iridescent scales. The tail is approximately 12 ft long, and is heavily muscular, weighing at approximately 200 pounds by itself. She uses the tail both offensively and defensively, wrapping herself in its embrace to protect herself from attacks or lashing out with the tail, or winding them around her prey and tightening to slowly crush them.

The Viper has partial heterochromia, her eyes containing both the colors of green and yellow. Her snake-like slit pupils expand and narrow as she wills.
