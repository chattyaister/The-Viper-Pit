Organization Overview

The Viper Pit has existed for 30 years, initially under different leaders and under the name of the Vermillion Pact. The organization was overthrown and taken over by Wisteria 10 or so years ago, and expanded rapidly from there. It is a rapidly growing syndicate situated in Mistral, the Viper Pit specializes in the production of their major drugs, “Venom”, and “Hush”, created after a long period of questionable experimentation carried out by the co-founder, Aluran

Primary Assets

The criminal organization deals mostly in their specialized drug “Venom” and “Hush” as well as information, most notably information of huntsmen and huntresses, students of huntsmen/huntress schools and villains alike.

The Viper Pit

The Viper Pit was founded by Wisteria Euphony and Aluran Nychta, after they slowly strangled the previous organization that stood in their path, the Vermillion Pact.

Vermillion Pact

Before the Viper Pit, was the Vermillion Pact. The Vermillion Pact was a crime institution run by a select group of figures that named themselves the Vermillion Hand. Operating under the guise of binding pacts, contracts, and agreements, they loosely justified the atrocities committed through the means of trades and obligations.

The Pact preyed on the addicted, the needy and the desperate- those who were willing to sign their futures away for immediate profit or relief. In their systems, the signed pact was law. Those who could or would not complete its terms resulted in the signee’s forcible collection from their homes, from whence they would be funneled into the black market- in parts, or as whole. Within years, the Pact grew within Mistral, becoming a structure of exploitation, a system of debt and ownership of people.

The downfall begins with a young woman from outside, and a young man from inside.

The Disappearances

When everyday, low-tier members within the Pact went missing, it was deemed insignificant. Disappearances within Mistral were far from uncommon, and the disappearances were isolated enough to not warrant immediate follow-up, simply deemed statistical noise.

The statistical noise began to repeat.

A courier, dead two districts from his designated path. A collector, dead face down in a pond outside his home. An enforcer, a contract handler, their family members. Each body was found with a single stalk of wisteria tucked into their hands. When the incidents began to compound, whispers began first in the lower ranking members of the Pact - of how a higher ranking member, someone in the Hand, had authorized the pruning of their own rankings and structures. Rumors that spread well, given the alternative would be that there was someone outside, that had learned their way into their systems.

When the next bodies were found, it was clear. The attacks were no longer random. It had become precise, calculated, targeted. Groups of members in specific contract lines went missing in meticulous order. High level enforcers, overseers, handlers, one after another members would turn up missing or dead, members whose absence didn’t disrupt just headcount, but uprooted entire systems within the institution.

The lower ranking members began to push back against the system that no longer protected them, refusing to be assigned to jobs that would have them alone, attempting to transfer branches to avoid a system they could not understand, or abandoning post entirely, preferring exile over… whatever this was, whatever was causing this madness.

Repetitive Methodology

The deaths began to resemble one another. Each body was found with strange abrasions upon their body, as if constricted. Whispers of a frequent sighting circulated- of a young, white- tailed faunus at the site of each death.

The lilac haired boy never interfered directly in the affairs of the Pact. He never fought, never lied, rather, he rerouted, altered truths, omitted facts. He specialized in truth- he simply decided who deserved the entirety of it. Information never quite made it fully intact, and what information made it to the correct people were half-truths and missing details, with the rest always arriving a hint too late. When information became untrustworthy, things began to rapidly spiral.

It was at this point, the Hand would attempt to correct itself.

Internal audits intensified, communications became restricted, members were reassigned into tighter clusters in an attempt to slow the spiral- yet the deaths continued. Not faster, not flashier, simply continued, at their usual pace, like she knew what she was doing.

He moved the pieces, and she hunted.

The institution began to dismantle itself in the guise of preservation, as members uproared about the new restrictions in place and their overall lack of action. And as the murders began to slowly climb higher and higher up the ladder, tensions mounted to a breaking point within the organization, creating a rift between the Hand and the everyday members.

Rising Tensions

When the tensions peaked, a report would make it to one of the hands desks. Implications were dire. This report would lead to a killing of one of the Hands by another.

The killings did not stop.

This led to the killing of the Hand who killed the other, as suspicions continued to mount in his involvement in the whole ordeal. When the killings yet again continued, the Hands would turn against each other, each suspecting the other.

The system grew more and more unstable as the Hands refused to communicate with one another, and the little trust they had begun to evaporate- When panic set in, drastic measures took place- The Hand began culling numbers from their system. And when a failed audit leads to the deaths of many innocent members, trust in the system finally breaks, and a riot would break out. Among the chaos, among rioting masses, was Wisteria, the girl with the lavender hair, and Aluran, the boy with the Lilac hair.

One,

Two,

The Hands fell to the masses, no longer able to contain the creature they had created. When the last went deep into hiding, it was the lilac boy that stood up from the crowd. He mapped the organization under the prying eyes of the mass, pointing out places apt for hiding in, hidden chambers within the quarters, passageways behind walls, every little hidden crevice in the building. It wouldn’t be long before the man would be found.

Execution

The lilac boy’s hand stopped the mass at the entrance way, allowing only the Lavender girl to enter. She slid slowly into the room, her iridescent scales shimmering in the light. The man cowered in the corner.

The little viper cooed at the cowering man, how weak he looked without his people, with all that power that kept him big stripped away, all alone. From her yellow-green eyes dripped curiosity, fascination, a quiet delight. Her tail slowly coiled around his ankle, pulling him close as her tail slid over him, winding prey. She walks, the man dragging behind her, towards the crowd.

She stops at the door, where many eyes watched, pulling the man into an upright position in front of them with a smile. She’d sink her teeth into the man's neck, her aura quickly bleeding into the man’s body. He’d convulse with an agonized scream from the vice of her tail.

Thud.

He was dropped from her grip, hitting the floor with a resounding thump, as he writhed about, his hands scratching and tearing at the site of his neck where the Viper’s teeth had sank. Face paled rapidly, veins bulging, eyes reddening- the room was filled with silence and screams.

“You may have him.”

Her voice cut through the man’s screams. No one moved a muscle, hesitation lingering in the air. No one could know if permission meant safety from… her. She smiled at the crowd with a quiet fascination.

The Pact was broken.

The process took 5 years.

Mistral remembers the wisteria.

New Regime

Under the new regime, the crime den would shed their former identity as the Vermillion Pact- and the Viper Pit rose from its ashes. The two- The Viper and the Doctor, worked meticulously together, with the Doctor bringing order into the fragmented organization, and the Viper bringing the terror that kept everyone in line.

Of course, there was some resistance met throughout the assimilation process. Resistance was swiftly met with blade and teeth from the Viper’s end. She’d display the bodies of the disloyal within the crime den hung up high, a stalk of wisteria tucked neatly into their pockets, a little reminder, to stay in line.

They were never dead.

The Doctor would reorganize routes to remove inefficiencies. Build reward systems for the loyal and competent. Establish hierarchy within the system. He, with ease, began managing many aspects of the organization, keeping himself busy. His methods were efficient and calculated, as if he had studied and understood the structure of the organization for years. He had.

Under the two, the shaky den would quickly stabilize, finding structure once again. Paychecks arrived on time, missing shipments became a rarity, territorial disputes were a thing of the past. They stayed, because in this efficient system, they would thrive as long as they stayed loyal and out of the Viper’s line of sight.

And when the den had regained its stable footing, and the task of overseeing well distributed among competent members, with order restored- the Doctor no longer needed to rebuild. He was free to create.

From the Ashes

He receded into the labs where his experiments would begin. He had ample subjects. The Vermillion hand had many bodies, those who had sold their lives away- and the Viper Pit had inherited every last one of them. He simply had to collect. The very people consumed by the pact became the foundation of the Viper Pit.

Some days there were screams.

Some days there was silence.

Some days silence followed screams.

The Doctor worked diligently day and night amid screams and silence, quietly creating and testing his concoctions within the confines of his lab. A way to enhance the body, so that people may not break. A way to numb the mind, so that people may not break. His goal was simple. Create people that do not break. He sought a solution to fragility. With this goal in mind he’d create two new drugs, that would come to be named “Venom” and “Hush.”

This would become their major source of income and power.

Hush was sold in large quantities for reasonable prices to the outskirts of cities and small towns where Grimm activity was abundant and towns were unprotected. People sought to use Hush to reduce the prevalence of Grimm attacks, and quell emotional distress in people, making the drug widespread and common.

Venom on the other hand, would not be released to the general public- instead sold only to the rich and powerful and desperate for an exorbitant price. Though the rebound effects were dire, the drug’s effectiveness at providing 30 minutes of clarity and superhuman capabilities makes the drug sought after.

With these two drugs, the Viper Pit would expand rapidly from their Home in Mistral, their dark tendrils expanding beneath the surface underneath many major kingdoms and cities of Remnant, maintaining a relatively positive facade thanks to the widespread and surprisingly effective nature of Hush at suppressing Grimm occurrences.

The drugs they create are widely debated- from the unknown method with which the drugs were concocted, to the ethics of abandoning emotions for the sake of preservation, to the devastating dependency that follows its use. Yet the facts cannot be denied- the drugs are effective. And therefore enticing.

Frontier towns that are often overrun by grimm swear up and down the effects of Hush, defending its use adamantly.
